Syntropia

For clinicians and researchers

Every intervention happens inside a process already underway. Something is moving; something else has stalled. Knowing which is which is what orients the work.

Syntropia is a tool for mapping that movement with precision.

It does so by attending to how a person exercises agency, connects with others, projects themselves forward in time, and grounds their sense of who they are. These dimensions don’t operate in isolation — the model formalizes how they shape one another.

The clinician gains a clearer picture of where a person actually stands: what’s in motion, what’s fixed, what’s within reach, and what isn’t.

Syntropia maps configurations, transitions, and paths — the organization, transformation, and unfolding of human experience.

It doesn’t compete with existing diagnostic frameworks. It works alongside them. What it adds is what categorical systems can’t generate from within their own architecture — dynamics, the missing variable.
