People Are More Than a Label

For a long time, we have described people as though a single word could define who they are.

Yet these ways of naming do more than describe a situation: they often transform a condition, a difficulty, or a pattern of behavior into a fixed identity.

This mode of thought—characteristic of substance ontology—has profoundly influenced clinical practice, education, and the way we come to understand ourselves.
